About

Dr. Sheen S. Levine investigates how people behave and how their behavior ripples through organizations and markets. His work bridges economics, sociology, psychology, and cognitive science — never content to stay within a single discipline when the most important questions live at their intersections.

His current research asks why diversity systematically improves error detection, when decision-makers choose the familiar over the genuinely promising, and how acts of generosity propagate through networks of strangers. The answers carry real consequences: executives at BlackRock and the City of New York have changed how they manage in direct response to his findings.

He has led research teams to publications in Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, Organization Science, and the Strategic Management Journal. His work has attracted attention from scholars across six disciplines, and from the public via two decades of media coverage spanning five continents.

Real-World Impact

New York City Pension Fund Reform
In 2015, New York City restructured the selection of managers for its $160 billion pension funds, explicitly citing Levine's research on diversity and decision-making as the basis for change.
Cross-Disciplinary Reach
Findings have been cited by scholars across six fields — business, economics, psychology, sociology, law, and computer science — reflecting research that transcends any single academic tradition.
Industry Collaboration
Research conducted in partnership with Bain & Company, BlackRock, Deloitte, and KPMG. Top executives said the work changed how they manage their organizations and assess risk.
